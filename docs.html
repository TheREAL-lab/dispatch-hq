Independent Technician Coordination Agreement

Texas Gate Dispatch — Dispatch Coordination Service

This Independent Technician Coordination Agreement (the “Agreement”) is entered into as of [DATE] between Taylor Keup, an individual doing business as “Texas Gate Dispatch” (“Coordinator”), and [TECHNICIAN / COMPANY LEGAL NAME] (“Technician”).

1. Nature of the Relationship

Technician is an independent business. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, agency, partnership, or joint-venture relationship. Technician controls the means, methods, tools, schedule, and pricing of Technician’s work, and is free to accept or decline any dispatch offered by Coordinator for any reason. Technician may work for any other party at any time.

2. Coordination Only; Direct Contracting

Coordinator operates a dispatch coordination service that connects self-storage facilities and similar commercial clients (each a “Facility”) with independent repair technicians. Coordinator does not perform, direct, supervise, inspect, or guarantee any repair work. All repair work is contracted directly between Technician and the Facility. Technician invoices the Facility directly, at Technician’s own rates, and collects payment directly from the Facility. Coordinator is not a party to that repair contract and at no time holds, transmits, or controls repair funds.

3. Coordination Fee

Coordinator charges each Facility a separate, flat coordination fee for dispatch services. Technician owes Coordinator nothing. There is no fee split, referral fee, commission, or deduction from Technician’s invoices under this Agreement.

4. Licenses and Legal Compliance

Technician represents and warrants that Technician holds, and will maintain in good standing, every license, registration, and permit required by law for the work Technician accepts — including, where applicable, licensure under the Texas Private Security Act (Tex. Occ. Code ch. 1702, administered by the Texas Department of Public Safety) for alarm, access-control, electronic-security, and related regulated work. Technician will not accept a dispatch for regulated work unless so licensed. Technician will notify Coordinator within 24 hours if any required license or registration lapses, or is suspended or revoked. Technician will comply with all applicable laws and with each Facility’s reasonable site rules.

5. Insurance

Technician will maintain commercial general liability insurance of at least [$1,000,000] per occurrence, plus automobile insurance as required by law, for the duration of this Agreement. Technician will provide Coordinator a current certificate of insurance on request and upon each renewal, and will notify Coordinator promptly of any cancellation or lapse in coverage.

6. Dispatch Standards

When Technician accepts a dispatch, Technician agrees to: (a) confirm an arrival window with Coordinator or the Facility; (b) give prompt notice of any delay; (c) photograph the completed work (and, where practical, the pre-repair condition); and (d) provide a brief completion status to Coordinator. Coordinator may track objective performance measures (such as on-time arrival, first-visit resolution, and Facility feedback) and may use them to prioritize future dispatch offers.

7. Warranty of Work

Any warranty on parts or workmanship runs solely from Technician to the Facility on Technician’s own terms. Coordinator makes no warranty regarding any repair and has no obligation to remedy any repair.

8. Non-Circumvention

For each Facility first introduced to Technician by Coordinator, Technician agrees, for twelve (12) months following the most recent dispatch at that Facility, to route new repair requests from that Facility through Coordinator rather than accepting them directly. This section does not restrict (a) warranty or call-back work on jobs previously dispatched, or (b) Facilities with which Technician had a documented relationship before this Agreement.

9. Indemnification

Technician will defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Coordinator from and against any third-party claim, damage, loss, or expense (including reasonable attorney’s fees) arising out of Technician’s work, acts, omissions, or breach of this Agreement.

10. Limitation of Liability

Coordinator’s total liability to Technician under this Agreement will not exceed one hundred dollars ($100). Neither party is liable to the other for indirect, incidental, consequential, or lost-profit damages.

11. Term and Termination

This Agreement begins on the date above and continues until either party terminates it with written notice (email suffices). Sections 7–10 and any accrued obligations survive termination.

12. General

This Agreement is governed by Texas law, with venue in Collin County, Texas. It is the entire agreement between the parties on this subject and may be amended only in a writing signed by both parties. Technician may not assign this Agreement without Coordinator’s consent. Coordinator may assign this Agreement to a business entity formed by Coordinator (including Texas Gate Dispatch LLC) upon its formation, without Technician’s consent. This Agreement may be signed in counterparts, and electronic signatures are effective.

DOC 02Facility Dispatch Coordination Terms

Dispatch Coordination Terms

Texas Gate Dispatch · [PHONE] · [EMAIL]

These Terms govern dispatch coordination services provided by Taylor Keup d/b/a Texas Gate Dispatch (“Coordinator”) to [FACILITY LEGAL NAME / LOCATION] (“Facility”). Requesting a dispatch after receiving these Terms constitutes acceptance, whether or not signed below.

1. The Service

Coordinator provides on-call coordination for gate, keypad, camera, and access-control repairs: intake of the issue, matching to an appropriate independent technician from Coordinator’s vetted roster, dispatch, and follow-up through completion.

2. Vetting

Before dispatch, Coordinator confirms each technician’s certificate of general liability insurance and — for work regulated under the Texas Private Security Act (Tex. Occ. Code ch. 1702) — verifies the technician’s Texas DPS license status in the state’s TOPS licensing system. Vetting confirms credentials; it is not a guarantee of workmanship.

3. Independent Technicians; Direct Billing

Each technician is an independent business, not Coordinator’s employee or agent. The repair itself is contracted directly between Facility and the technician: the technician quotes their own rates, performs the work, warrants the work on their own terms, and invoices Facility directly. Coordinator does not perform or supervise repairs and never handles repair funds.

4. Coordination Fee

Facility pays Coordinator a flat coordination fee of [$89] per dispatch. The fee is earned when the assigned technician arrives at the Facility. No fee is due if Coordinator cannot source a technician or the technician fails to arrive. Invoices are due net fifteen (15) days.

5. Liability

Facility’s remedy for any issue with repair work lies against the technician who performed it. Coordinator’s total liability to Facility will not exceed the coordination fees paid by Facility in the three (3) months before the claim. Neither party is liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or lost-profit damages.

6. General

Either party may stop using the service at any time; accrued fees remain payable. These Terms are governed by Texas law, venue in Collin County, Texas, and may be assigned by Coordinator to a business entity formed by Coordinator (including Texas Gate Dispatch LLC) upon its formation.

DOC 04Technician Onboarding Checklist

Welcome to the Dispatch Roster

Texas Gate Dispatch — Technician Onboarding

We coordinate gate, keypad, camera, and access-control repair calls for self-storage facilities across North DFW and send them to vetted independent techs. Here’s the deal, plainly:

  • You never pay us anything. The facility pays our coordination fee; you bill the facility directly at your own rates and keep 100%.
  • You choose your jobs. Every dispatch is offered by text — accept or pass, no penalty.
  • Reliability gets rewarded. On-time arrivals and first-visit fixes move you to the top of the call list.

Send us these five things to go active

  • Certificate of insurance — commercial general liability, $1M per occurrence.
  • Texas DPS license number — required only if you take alarm / access-control / electronic security work (we verify status in the state TOPS system). Mechanical-gate-only techs can skip this.
  • Signed Coordination Agreement (we’ll send it — two pages, plain English).
  • Your coverage — service area, trades you take (gate mechanical, gate operators, keypads, access control, cameras), and typical service-call rate so we can quote facilities accurately.
  • Best cell number for dispatch texts.

How a dispatch works

  1. We text you the facility, the equipment, and the issue.
  2. You accept and give us an arrival window; we confirm with the facility.
  3. You do the work, snap photos of the completed repair, and text us “done.”
  4. You invoice the facility directly and get paid on your terms.
